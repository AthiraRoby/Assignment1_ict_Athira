Let's explore through the 14 districts of God's own Country, KERALA



THIRUVANANTHAPURAM

According to legend, the site of Thiruvananthapuram was once a jungle known as Anantan Kādu, which was home to a Pulayar couple who cultivated rice. Explore more this ‘the city of Lord Anantha’Know more

KOLLAM

Kollam is a coastal town famous for its delicious and lip-smacking seafood. The town is flanked with plethora of good restaurants all offering the authentic traditional flavors of Kerala including that of crab, prawns, fish and squids. Know more

ALAPUZHA

Alappuzha has a diverse topography. It is a sandy strip of land intercepted by lagoons, rivers and canals.A place to explore and enjoy.Know more

PATHANAMTHITTA

Pathanamthitta is a district in Kerala known for its natural beauty as well as for fairs and festivals. Pathanamthitta is situated on the banks of Achankovil River. Because of its scenic beauty, a trip to Pathanamthitta would undoubtedly be an unforgettable experience.Know more


IDUKI

A proud home of South India’s highest peak, Anamudi that stands at a height of 2,695 meters, Idukki is one of the most stunning hill stations in Kerala. Know more

KOTTAYAM

In all its natural glory, Kottayam is a magnificently breathtaking place with green stretches of landscapes and hills that are often clad in snowy mists-all of which will leave you gasping for breath.Know more

ERNAKULAM

This modern city one of the prime attractions in Kerala, like beautiful coastlines, fringed with coconut and palm trees, backwaters, that leads to peaceful and sleepy villages of Kerala, and enormous natural beauty. Know more

TRISSUR

The term Thrissur abbreviated form Malayalam word ‘THRISSIVAPERUR’ which means the town of the “SACRED SIVA”. The town is built on an elevated ground, at the apex of which is the famous “VADAKKUMNATHAN” Temple. A place of great antiquity.Know more


PALAKKAD

The district of Palakkad is the traditional gateway to Kerala from the north. One of the larger districts of Kerala, Palakkad has a topography that is a blend of forest cover, rivers, valleys, hills etc. It is also one of the regions in Kerala having a tribal populationKnow more

MALAPPURAM

Bounded by the Nilgiris hills on the east and the Arabian Sea on the west, the land atop the hills, is remarkable for its unique natural beauty. Perched among the undulating hills and the rivers that flow to reach the coconut-fringed seacoast, the land conceals a unique history.Know more

KOZHIKODE

This District was at one time the capital of the powerful Zamorins and a prominent trade and commerce center, Kozhikode was the most important region of Malabar in the days gone by.Know more

WAYANADU

The green paradise is nestled among the mountains of the Western Ghats, forming the border world of the greener part of Kerala. Clean and pristine, enchanting and hypnotizing, this land is filled with history and culture. Know more


KANNUR

This district derived its name from the location of its headquarters at Kannur town. Kannur was formerly known as Cannanore. Kannur is popularly known as the city of Looms and Lores. Kannur is the land of the famous performing art Theyyam.Know more

KASARGOD

It is mainly known all over the world for its coir and handloom industries. Kasaragod is also known as the land of gods, forts, temples, rivers, hills and beautiful beaches. This place is like a heaven where nature still plays an important part in the everyday life.Know more
